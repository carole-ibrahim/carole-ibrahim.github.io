                    <h3>Diversified recommendations of cultural activities with personalized determinantal point processes</h3>
                    <p class="publication-authors">C Ibrahim, H Bederina, D Cuesta, L Montier, C Delabre, JJ Vie</p>
                    <p class="publication-journal">International Workshop on Recommender Systems for Sustainability and Social Good</p>
                    <p class="publication-abstract">While optimizing recommendation systems for user engagement is a well-established practice, effectively diversifying recommendations without negatively impacting core business metrics remains a significant industry challenge. In line with our initiative to broaden our audienceâ€™s cultural practices, this study investigates using personalized Determinantal Point Processes (DPPs) to sample diverse and relevant recommendations. We rely on a well-known quality-diversity decomposition of the similarity kernel to give more weight to user preferences. In this paper, we present our implementations of the personalized DPP sampling, evaluate the trade-offs between relevance and diversity through both offline and online metrics, and give insights for practitioners on their use in a production environment. For the sake of reproducibility, we release the full code for our platform and experiments on GitHub.</p>
